Squat · Barbell

Focus Area

Legs, Glutes, Core

Execution

Lower hips until thighs are parallel, then push through heels to stand tall.

Bench Press · Barbell

Focus Area

Chest, Shoulders, Triceps

Execution

Lower the bar to chest level, press upward with controlled power.

Deadlift · Barbell

Focus Area

Back, Glutes, Hamstrings

Execution

Keep spine neutral, lift bar smoothly using your legs and hips.

Deadlift · Barbell

Focus Area

Back, Glutes, Hamstrings

Execution

Keep spine neutral, lift bar smoothly using your legs and hips.

Pull-Up · Bodyweight

Focus Area

Back, Biceps, Core

Execution

Grip the bar shoulder-width, pull up until your chin clears it, then lower slowly.

Bent-Over Row · Barbell

Focus Area

Back, Lats, Rear Delts

Execution

Maintain a flat back, pull the bar towards your abdomen, and lower under control.

Shoulder Press · Dumbbell

Focus Area

Shoulders, Triceps, Core

Execution

Press dumbbells overhead, pause, and lower with control to shoulder height.

Lunges · Dumbbell

Focus Area

Legs, Glutes, Balance

Execution

Step forward, drop the back knee near the ground, and push through your front heel to return.

Bicep Curl · Dumbbell

Focus Area

Biceps, Forearms

Execution

Keep elbows close to torso, curl dumbbells up, and slowly lower back down.

Dips · Bodyweight

Focus Area

Chest, Triceps, Shoulders

Execution

Lower body by bending elbows until arms form 90°, then press up powerfully.

Plank Hold · Core

Focus Area

Core, Abs, Shoulders

Execution

Keep your body straight, engage core muscles, and hold position steadily.

Importance of Diet in Weight Gain

Building muscle and gaining healthy weight isn’t just about lifting heavier — it’s about fueling your body right. A calorie surplus rich in proteins, healthy fats, and complex carbohydrates helps your muscles recover and grow stronger. Balanced nutrition ensures your workouts actually transform into visible gains, not just sweat and effort.
